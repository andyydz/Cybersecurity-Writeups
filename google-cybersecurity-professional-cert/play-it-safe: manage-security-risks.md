## Reflection

This course strengthened my understanding of how organizations identify, assess, and manage cybersecurity risks. I learned how security analysts evaluate threats, vulnerabilities, and business risks using industry-recognized frameworks such as the NIST Risk Management Framework (RMF) and the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF).

Throughout the course, I explored the importance of the CIA Triad (Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability) in designing secure systems and protecting organizational assets. I also gained an understanding of OWASP Security Principles, security controls, compliance requirements, and the role of security audits in improving an organization's overall security posture.

A significant part of this course focused on Security Information and Event Management (SIEM). I learned how SIEM platforms collect, centralize, and analyze security logs to detect suspicious activities, monitor security events, and support incident investigations. In addition, I explored the fundamentals of Security Orchestration, Automation, and Response (SOAR) and how automation helps security teams respond to incidents more efficiently.

The course also introduced structured incident response processes, including the use of playbooks to investigate, contain, eradicate, and recover from security incidents. Through portfolio activities and practical exercises, I developed a stronger understanding of how SOC analysts apply security frameworks, analyze risks, investigate alerts, and support organizational security operations.

Overall, this course provided a solid foundation in defensive cybersecurity and strengthened my knowledge of risk management, security monitoring, and incident response within a modern Security Operations Center (SOC).
